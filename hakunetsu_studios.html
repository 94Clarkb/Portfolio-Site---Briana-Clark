Hakunetsu Studios - Responsive Web-App


This web-app is designed to be a merchandise e-commerce shop for those looking for things relating to Hakunetsu Studios’s universe of books, webtoons, anime, video games and merchandise. Anyone and everyone can find something that fits their taste.

Project Objective

Help everyone find products quickly and easily to match their particular needs.


My Role

UX/UI Designer responsible for bringing this project from conception to delivery.


Project Duration

September 2024 - October 2024


Responsibilities and Deliverables

  • 5Ws
  • User Flow Diagram
  • Wireframes
  • User Testing Notes and Results
  • Desktop Design
  • Mockups

5Ws


The project brief provided me with the 5ws already established. I wanted to include it in the process now because it is a guiding light through the rest of the project. Remembering the 5ws through the entirety of the project ensures that I can remain focused on the direction the web-app will take.

User Stories

  1. “As a new customer, I want to access the inventory without having to register, so that I can make sure this store has what I’m looking for before having to create an account.”
  2. “As a customer, I want there to be a variety of payment options, so that I can choose the payment method that suits me best.”
  3. “As a returning customer, I want to be able to save items that I cannot buy to a wish list, so that I can purchase them at a later date.”

Empathy is a driving force in UI and UX. It is an important thing to have and when I read these statements provided by the brief, I knew choosing only three would be challenging. I resonated with all of them, but these three stood out to me. I felt like these were common problems people have everyday with shopping online, but I personally felt these on a more personal level and could relate to the users when they shared their needs.

MVP Objective

This web-app is designed to be a merchandise e-commerce shop for those looking for things relating to Hakunetsu Studios’s universe of books, webtoons, anime, video games and merchandise. Anyone and everyone can find something that fits their taste.

The MVP Objective was something I came up with next. After choosing the user stories and revisiting the 5Ws, I made the objective for the web-app. This is what the web-app is, and who it’s for. Its purpose.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

The next thing I did was ideate by creating Low Fidelity wireframes. It is usually seen that user flows come before the wireframes, but I found that it was difficult for my ideas to come through unless I were to draw them out. This became part of my process, and is something unique to me and my workflow. After ideating and seeing everything put down onto canvas, I was then able to create the user flow more efficiently.


User Flow


After the Low-Fidelity wireframes, I was able to come to the User Flow with a clear head. This ensured that the User Flow captured the exact direction I wanted the web-app to take, with plenty of room to adjust as needed later on. It was a challenge for me to do this process prior to the ideation phase with Low-Fidelity wireframes because I wasn’t able to think without seeing visually what the site would look like should it be built up. Only then could I see it take form and give me a better opportunity to design with a clear head.
