Hello, my name is Briana Clark. Due to the various pronunciations of my name, I also go by Bri (pronounced like "Brie" cheese, without the 'e') for ease of communication.

I am a UI/UX Designer and Developer with a diverse background spanning Psychology, Healthcare, Education, and Retail. Each of these fields has contributed invaluable experience to my skill set.

I began my academic journey with an Associate’s degree in Psychology, initially aspiring to become a psychiatrist. To gain hands-on experience with early childhood development, I taught in a Montessori setting, where I observed the alignment between children’s growth and my academic studies. Seeking to advance my career, I transitioned into the medical field, working as a Licensed Pharmacy Technician in both Retail and Hospital Inpatient Pharmacy. This experience broadened my perspective but ultimately led me to reassess my career goals.

I soon found myself exploring Pharmacy programs with the intent of becoming a pharmacist. Meanwhile, my husband, a software engineer, introduced me to the world of coding. His focus was on backend development, but he encouraged me to explore frontend development, which aligned more closely with my creative interests. This discovery ignited my passion for UI/UX Design and Development, leading to a significant career shift away from healthcare.

Creativity has always been a driving force in my life, evolving from a childhood escape into a lifelong passion. After working in roles focused on memorization and rapid decision-making, I yearned for the creative challenges that design and development offers. I find great satisfaction in navigating design puzzles and experiencing the “lightbulb” moments that spark a new idea or project. While I enjoy working on my own creative tasks, and can work well independently, I also thrive in environments where I can collaborate with others on different decisions relating to our projects. Feedback is debatably one of the most valuable things when designing solutions for others.

UI/UX design enables me to serve others while pursuing my passion for creativity. My aspiration is to develop solutions that foster comfort, inspiration, and a sense of “coziness” in daily life, as I believe that life should not be overwhelmingly stressful all the time.

In addition to my Psychology degree, I have completed the CareerFoundry UI Design Certification course, equipping me with the knowledge necessary to excel in this dynamic industry.
